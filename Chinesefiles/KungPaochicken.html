Description: Spring rolls are a popular Asian snack or appetizer, typically consisting of a thin, crispy wrapper filled with a variety of ingredients. There are two main types: fresh and fried. Fresh spring rolls are made with translucent rice paper and are often filled with fresh vegetables, shrimp, herbs, and vermicelli noodles, served cold with dipping sauces like peanut or hoisin sauce. Fried spring rolls, on the other hand, are wrapped in a wheat-based dough and deep-fried until golden and crispy, with fillings that can include ground pork, shrimp, cabbage, and mushrooms. Both versions are loved for their combination of textures, from the crunch of the wrapper to the savory fillings inside, making them a versatile and flavorful dish.
